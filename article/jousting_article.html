Knights Ready'd for Jousting in Dotheim

The greatest of tournaments in the lande of Wescoln shall take place in Dotheim this forthcoming weekende.

Sir Knights of Dotheim and Straits ride as one in fellowshipe.

by Anezka Stankar

Updated 56 minutes ago

The grande jousting tournament in Wescoln doth come to Dotheim this weekende. Normally held in stately Straits, the good burghers of Dotheim are in a state of bewilderement. They arst divesersely beset by eagerness whilst others worry about the amount of gawkers shall accost their fair village.

The most contentious matter, however, doth revolve around the question of who amongst the knights shall claim victory, and the taverns arst full of wavering.

In the last year, the tournament drew a thousand gawkers to Straits. Yet, Sir Jirka Habsburg doth avow that this year's spectacle shall prove a grand success, and more particularly, a wondrous boon unto himself.

– Mine honour demandeth blood, says former champion of jousting Sir Jirka Habsburg in an interview unto us, The Post. A somewhat theatrical claim considering he hath not but one a trophy this past year after he lost his jousting arme.

Habsburg arrived early this dawn after three days of travailling. Verily, he doth express no discontent with longe distance to Dotheim, but rather, doth boast of certitude in the forthcoming tournament. Quoth he, when asked of his arme's plight:

– I thank the Almighty for this accidente. The loss of mine right arme has only made my other arme twice as stronge, he says with certain confidens.

Verily, we shall see he doth bringe forth. Equestriane commenter Ydris Svoboda hath previously spoken his doubts of Habsburg’s future. The former champion of jousting hath three trophies of under his, wellst, former arme, but none hath come to him after the jousting accidente in 1423.

– I remember as if’twere but yesterdaye. I have never beheld a limb depart from the body like that. The valor in returning, even in the face of severance, is praiseworthy, yet I doubt the Sir shall fare well this year, says Ydris Svoboda when inquired about Habsburg’s accidente.

Habsburg’s biggest foe is fair Tereza Cerny, who doth intend to contest in the tournament of Dotheim. Alas, she hath not yet arrived, but doth purpose to appear on the morn of the tomorrow.

– My wages arst vested in Tereza. Nay, seriously. I beteth my son’s sole horse, says tavernkeeper Josef.

Josef might not be completely off; for Cerny hath four trophies, and two of them originate fromst this past year. Many a gent takes wagers with Josef, for Cerny hath already become the champion most wagered upon since the Great War.

We shall see whether Cerny ist able to withstand Habsburg’s left arme, however.
